What EnrichLayer is — and where it's strong

EnrichLayer (enrichlayer.com) is a developer-first B2B enrichment API with a unified REST interface covering people, companies, jobs, and contact data. There's no point-and-click UI and no CRM workflows: you write code, or you don't use it. For indie developers and product teams who want cheap, raw enrichment at the API layer, that focus is a genuine strength, and the per-credit pricing is competitive at scale.

It's also fair to note where EnrichLayer came from. Its endpoint structure and credit model closely mirror Proxycurl — the LinkedIn data API that shut down in 2025 after a LinkedIn lawsuit — and EnrichLayer is widely described as a Proxycurl-style successor that stepped into the gap. We're not going to assert it's the exact same company, because that hasn't been publicly confirmed. What matters for your decision is the design it inherited and the trade-offs that come with it.

The short version: EnrichLayer wins on cheap raw per-credit cost at volume. LinkFinder AI wins on lead discovery (natural-language search vs. a few filters), pricing simplicity (1 credit = 1 request, monthly, no annual lock-in), and an architecture that doesn't depend on LinkedIn accounts.

EnrichLayer pricing, and the real cost per record

EnrichLayer's plans are annual and credit-based, with pay-as-you-go also available. Here are the public 2026 tiers:

PlanCredits / yearPer creditAnnual cost
Starter~35,000~$0.0168~$588
Growth~350,000~$0.0103~$3,588
Pro~1,258,600~$0.0086~$10,788
Ultra~2,954,000~$0.0077~$22,788

The headline per-credit number understates real cost, because endpoints consume credits at different rates: a person profile is 1 credit, a company lookup 2, a work email 3, and search burns 3 credits per result returned. So a Starter-tier work-email lookup is closer to $0.05 than $0.0168 — still cheap, but not the sticker number. The 500 trial credits work out to roughly 166 email lookups: enough to test, not to run a campaign.

Two things to weigh. EnrichLayer's best rates require an annual commitment, and its default architecture is cache-first — you tend to get cached records unless you explicitly request a live pull at higher credit cost. If predictable monthly spend and fresh-on-demand data matter to you, that's worth factoring in.

LinkFinder AI keeps it flat: 1 credit = 1 request regardless of endpoint, billed monthly, with 100 free credits to start. See the pricing page for current plan limits.

EnrichLayer: honest pros and cons

Where EnrichLayer is strong

  • Very low per-credit cost, especially at high volume
  • Clean, unified REST API across people, companies, jobs, contacts
  • Extensive granular data points per record
  • Pay-as-you-go option alongside annual plans
  • Genuinely developer-first — minimal abstraction, you control everything

Where it falls short

  • Best pricing locked behind an annual commitment
  • Cache-first by default — freshness costs extra credits
  • Few search filters (≈3 company / 5 people) — weak for discovery
  • API-only — no UI for non-technical teammates
  • Little third-party review presence or published accuracy benchmarks yet
  • Built on the LinkedIn-data approach that drew legal action against Proxycurl

Which one fits you?

Stay with EnrichLayer if…

You're a developer who only needs cheap raw enrichment of profiles you already have, you're comfortable API-only, you can commit annually, and you don't need rich search filters or a UI for the rest of your team. At very high volume, EnrichLayer's per-credit economics are hard to beat.

Switch to LinkFinder AI if…

You want to find leads, not just enrich known ones — describing an audience in plain English and getting back matching profiles. You'd rather pay monthly than lock into a year. You want one predictable credit cost per request instead of doing per-endpoint credit math. You want non-developers to use a UI while developers use the same API. And you'd prefer an architecture that doesn't depend on LinkedIn accounts.

EnrichLayer alternative FAQ

What is EnrichLayer?
EnrichLayer (enrichlayer.com) is a developer-first B2B enrichment API with a unified REST interface for people, companies, jobs, and contact data. It's API-only — no UI, no CRM workflows — and uses the same endpoint structure and credit pricing model Proxycurl popularized before Proxycurl shut down in 2025.
Is EnrichLayer the same as Proxycurl?
It's widely described as a Proxycurl-style successor and mirrors Proxycurl's endpoints and per-credit pricing. Whether it's run by the same company hasn't been publicly confirmed, so the accurate description is that it filled the gap Proxycurl left after its 2025 shutdown.
How much does EnrichLayer cost in 2026?
Annual, credit-based plans: Starter ~$588/yr (~35,000 credits, ~$0.0168/credit), scaling through Growth (~$3,588), Pro (~$10,788), and Ultra (~$22,788), plus 500 free trial credits. Because endpoints cost 1–3 credits (and search costs 3 per result), the effective cost per record runs higher than the headline rate.
What's a good alternative to EnrichLayer?
LinkFinder AI, if you want natural-language lead search instead of a few filters, flat 1-credit-per-request pricing, monthly billing instead of an annual commitment, and an architecture that doesn't rely on LinkedIn accounts. EnrichLayer stays reasonable for cheap raw per-credit profile enrichment at high volume.
Does EnrichLayer have a free plan?
It offers 500 free trial credits, not a permanent free tier; the cheapest paid plan is about $588/year. LinkFinder AI gives new accounts 100 free credits and bills monthly. See the pricing page.
How is LinkFinder AI's lead search different?
EnrichLayer's search is filter-based with a small number of filters. LinkFinder's AI Lead Finder takes a plain-English description (e.g. "Head of Marketing at e-commerce company in Europe, 50–200 employees") and returns up to 100 matching profiles per call, 1 credit each.
Does LinkFinder AI use my LinkedIn account?
No. B2B Data Lookup retrieves business-contact and company data without using or requiring your LinkedIn account, so there's no account-ban risk to you.
Is it hard to migrate from EnrichLayer?
No. Both are REST APIs that accept JSON. Point requests at https://api.linkfinderai.com, set the type field, pass your key as a Bearer token, and read status/result. Most integrations move in an afternoon — see the API documentation.

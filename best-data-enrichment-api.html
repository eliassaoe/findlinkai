12 Best Data Enrichment APIs in 2026

Looking for the best data enrichment API? We compared the top contact, company, and email enrichment providers to help you append clean, current data to the records you already have — for lead enrichment, CRM hygiene, and automation, without overpaying for stale fields.

"Data enrichment API" sounds like one thing, but buyers are actually shopping for three. Some providers specialize in contact enrichment (appending verified work emails and phone numbers to a name or domain), some in firmographic enrichment (filling in company size, industry, revenue, and tech stack), and some in identity resolution (stitching partial records into a single, deduplicated profile). They all advertise huge databases and high match rates — the real differences are in freshness, accuracy, pricing structure, and how cleanly you can build on top of them.

The pricing models are all over the map. Enrichment APIs typically charge per call or per matched record; some bill only on a hit, others on every lookup; enterprise platforms wrap everything in an annual contract. And match rate matters more than raw size: an API that returns a verified, current email for 80% of your list beats one with a billion records that only matches half of them — or worse, matches stale ones.

We compared the enrichment providers that actually deliver in 2026 across what matters most: enrichment type and coverage, match rate, data freshness, developer experience, and real starting price. The 12 below are the ones worth your time. The short version: if your goal is enriching contacts and companies with predictable, flat pricing and an API on every plan, you can skip the credit calculators and enterprise contracts entirely.

#2

Clearbit (HubSpot Breeze Intelligence)

Real-Time Firmographic Enrichment, Native to HubSpot

Clearbit was acquired by HubSpot in 2023 and rebranded as Breeze Intelligence. It's now the default enrichment layer inside the HubSpot ecosystem, offering real-time enrichment on form submission, company and contact append, and IP-based website visitor de-anonymization — long considered one of the cleanest firmographic enrichment APIs around.

If your entire go-to-market stack runs on HubSpot, Breeze is the most seamless way to enrich inbound leads and power lead scoring — zero integration friction. Pricing is credit-based: roughly $30 for 100 credits, $150 for 1,000, and $700 for 10,000 per month. The catch is that it's increasingly tied to HubSpot, so it's a weaker fit if you're not already in that ecosystem, and auto-renewal terms draw complaints.

Key Features

  • Real-time form enrichment - Append fields on submission
  • Company & contact append - Firmographic and role data
  • Visitor de-anonymization - IP-level company identification
  • HubSpot-native - Zero integration friction inside HubSpot
  • Lead scoring - Auto-score on enriched attributes

Credit packs – about $30 / 100 credits, $150 / 1,000, and $700 / 10,000 per month. Best value when bundled with HubSpot Marketing or Sales Hub.

✓ Pros

  • Seamless for HubSpot users
  • Strong real-time firmographic data
  • Visitor identification built in
  • Clean, well-known API heritage

✗ Cons

  • Increasingly locked to the HubSpot ecosystem
  • Less compelling outside HubSpot
  • Auto-renewal terms draw complaints
  • Lighter on direct contact (email/phone) data
#3

People Data Labs

Massive Person & Company Enrichment API

People Data Labs (PDL) is a developer-first enrichment provider with one of the largest person and company datasets around, delivered through clean enrichment and search APIs. Give it an identifier and it returns a structured profile; run a search and it returns matching records — straightforward to integrate into apps and CRMs.

Pricing is credit-based and transparent: person enrichment lands around $0.28 per record and drops toward $0.20 at scale, with company enrichment cheaper. A Pro plan starts near $98/month and there's a free tier with 100 credits to test. The trade-off versus live sources is freshness — because PDL serves a compiled dataset, some records can lag behind a person's current role.

Key Features

  • Huge person & company dataset - Among the largest available
  • Enrichment & search APIs - Person and company endpoints
  • Transparent per-record pricing - Roughly $0.28/record, lower at scale
  • Free tier - 100 credits to evaluate
  • Developer-friendly - Clean schema, best-in-class docs
  • Bulk + streaming delivery - Built for volume pipelines

Pro plan from $98/month (~350 enrichment credits) – or pay-as-you-go at ~$0.28 per person record, ~$0.10 per company record, dropping at volume. Free tier with 100 credits.

✓ Pros

  • Enormous dataset coverage
  • Clean, predictable per-record pricing
  • Generous free tier for testing
  • Great for high-volume enrichment
  • Excellent developer experience

✗ Cons

  • Some records can be stale vs. live data

  • Email/phone coverage varies by record
  • No UI or workflow layer — API only
#4

Apollo.io

All-in-One Database, Enrichment & Outreach

Apollo combines a 275M+ contact database with built-in email sequences, a dialer, and an enrichment API. For teams that want data and a way to act on it in one place, the all-in-one value often beats having a deeper enrichment endpoint you can't easily action.

Apollo's pricing makes enrichment accessible to teams that can't afford ZoomInfo — there's a free plan with 1,200 annual credits to test data quality, and Basic starts around $49/user/month. The trade-offs: enrichment isn't as deep as ZoomInfo's, mobile number accuracy lags Cognism, and the "unlimited" email credits are subject to fair-use limits that can throttle high-volume enrichment.

Key Features

  • 275M+ contact database - Broad person and company coverage
  • Enrichment API - Append emails, titles, and firmographics
  • Built-in outreach - Sequences, dialer, and LinkedIn automation
  • Free plan - 1,200 annual credits to test before paying
  • CRM sync - Native integrations with major CRMs

Free plan (1,200 annual credits) – Basic from ~$49/user/month, with mobile and export credit caps. Higher tiers add API volume and advanced features.

✓ Pros

  • Data + outreach in one platform
  • Accessible pricing and a real free tier
  • Large contact database
  • Good for SMB and mid-market sales

✗ Cons

  • Enrichment depth secondary to outreach
  • Mobile number accuracy is weaker

#5

Hunter.io

Email Finding & Verification, Done Simply

Hunter is the go-to for one specific enrichment job: turning a name and domain into a verified work email. Its Email Finder, Domain Search, and Email Verifier endpoints are clean, fast, and well documented, making it a favorite for developers who need reliable email enrichment without buying a whole sales platform.

The appeal is focus and price: there's a free tier for light use, and paid plans start around $34/month with predictable monthly credits. The trade-off is scope — Hunter is excellent at emails and email verification but isn't a deep firmographic or phone-number source, so larger operations often pair it with a broader enrichment provider.

Key Features

  • Email Finder API - Name + domain to a verified work email
  • Domain Search - Find email patterns and contacts for a company
  • Email Verifier - Validate deliverability before sending
  • Clean REST API - Simple, well-documented endpoints
  • Free tier - Monthly searches and verifications to start

Free tier available – paid plans from around $34/month with monthly search and verification credits. Higher tiers add volume and team seats.

✓ Pros

  • Excellent, focused email accuracy
  • Built-in verification reduces bounces
  • Very developer-friendly API
  • Affordable with a real free tier

✗ Cons

  • Narrow scope — emails, not full profiles
  • Light on phone and firmographic data
  • Credit caps pinch at high volume
  • Pair with another source for depth
#6

Clay

Orchestration Layer That Waterfalls 100+ Data Sources

Clay isn't a single dataset — it's an orchestration platform that connects 100+ enrichment providers (including Clearbit, Apollo, Hunter, Lusha, and more) and lets you build "waterfalls" that try multiple sources in sequence until you get the field you need. It's become a favorite for RevOps teams that want maximum match rate without committing to one vendor.

The model is powerful: you pay for credits and route each enrichment through the cheapest source that has the data, which can dramatically lift coverage. The trade-offs are a real learning curve, costs that climb with complex waterfalls, and the fact that you're still paying the underlying providers' credit costs on top of Clay's.

Key Features

  • 100+ connected sources - Clearbit, Apollo, Hunter, Lusha, and more
  • Waterfall enrichment - Try providers in sequence for max coverage
  • Spreadsheet-style UI - Build workflows without heavy code
  • AI research agents - Automate custom data gathering
  • Generous free tier - Test before scaling

Free tier available – paid plans from around $149/month, plus the underlying providers' credit costs. Cost scales with waterfall complexity and volume.

✓ Pros

  • Highest possible match rates via waterfalls
  • No single-vendor lock-in
  • Flexible, automatable workflows
  • Strong free tier to start

✗ Cons

  • Real learning curve
  • Costs stack (Clay + provider credits)
  • Can get expensive at scale
  • Not a data source itself
#7

ZoomInfo

The Enterprise Standard for Breadth + Intent

ZoomInfo is the enterprise heavyweight, combining one of the largest B2B databases (260M+ professional profiles, 100M+ company records) with intent data, website visitor identification, and workflow automation. Its enrichment API is comprehensive, but it's gated behind enterprise contracts.

If you need the broadest North American coverage and have the budget for large-scale enrichment or ABM, ZoomInfo is hard to beat on sheer breadth and its intent layer. The downsides are well documented: pricing starts around $15,000/year and most mid-market teams land at $25K–$40K after seats and modules, contracts are annual with narrow cancellation windows, and it's overkill for lean teams that just need to append a few fields.

Key Features

  • 260M+ profiles, 100M+ companies - Market-leading breadth
  • Intent data - Prioritize accounts showing buying behavior
  • Visitor identification - De-anonymize website traffic
  • Comprehensive enrichment API - Full contact and company append
  • CRM & workflow automation - Enterprise GTM tooling

From ~$15,000/year (SalesOS Professional) – most mid-market teams land at $25K–$40K annually after seats and add-ons. Quote-based, annual contracts.

✓ Pros

  • Largest North American B2B dataset
  • Built-in intent and visitor data
  • Comprehensive, enterprise-grade API
  • Strong CRM and automation tooling

✗ Cons

  • Expensive; five-figure annual minimum
  • Annual contracts with narrow cancellation
  • Overkill for SMBs and startups
  • API locked behind enterprise deals

Lusha

Lightweight Contact Enrichment for SMBs

Lusha is a lightweight, SMB-friendly contact data tool best known for its Chrome extension and quick email/phone lookups. It also offers an enrichment API for appending contact details to CRM records, with a low entry price that makes it approachable for small teams and individual reps.

The appeal is simplicity and cost: plans start around $29–$36/month and there's a free tier for light use. The trade-offs are depth and scale — coverage and firmographic richness trail the enterprise providers, credit caps can pinch high-volume users, and it's more of a point tool than an enrichment backbone for a large operation.

Key Features

  • Chrome extension - Quick lookups while browsing
  • Contact enrichment API - Append emails and phone numbers
  • CRM integrations - Push data into major CRMs
  • Low entry price - Accessible for SMBs and individuals
  • Free tier - Light monthly credits to start

From ~$29/month – credit-based, with a free tier for light use. Higher tiers add credits and team features.

✓ Pros

  • Very affordable entry point
  • Simple, fast to adopt
  • Good Chrome extension
  • Free tier for testing

✗ Cons

#9

Cognism

GDPR-Verified Mobile Data for EMEA Enrichment

Cognism is the go-to enrichment provider for teams selling into Europe, with strong GDPR-compliant coverage and phone-verified mobile numbers (its "Diamond Data"). For cold-calling-heavy SDR teams tired of disconnected numbers, the verified-mobile enrichment angle is the main draw.

It pairs solid contact data with intent signals (via a Bombora partnership) and comprehensive European coverage. The trade-offs are commercial: North American coverage isn't as deep as ZoomInfo's, pricing is fully quote-based (platform fee around €15,000/year plus per-seat costs), and you're negotiating blind without public tiers.

Key Features

  • Phone-verified mobiles - "Diamond Data" for higher connect rates
  • GDPR-compliant coverage - Strong EMEA data and compliance posture
  • Intent data - Buyer signals via Bombora partnership
  • CRM sync - Keep enriched records fresh automatically
  • Global coverage - Strongest in Europe

Quote-based – platform fee around €15,000/year plus roughly €1,500/user/year; phone-verified "Diamond" data adds more. Expect to negotiate.

✓ Pros

  • Best-in-class EMEA and mobile data
  • GDPR-verified, compliance-friendly
  • Intent signals included
  • High phone connect rates

✗ Cons

  • North American coverage trails ZoomInfo
  • Quote-based, opaque pricing
  • Platform + per-seat fees add up
  • Negotiation required to start
#10

Coresignal

AI-Ready Employee, Company & Job Datasets via API

Coresignal is an API-first data platform aimed at engineers, analysts, and AI teams. It collects from 15+ public sources and delivers structured, deduplicated records — hundreds of millions of employee profiles plus tens of millions of company records, refreshed multiple times a day — ideal when enrichment feeds a data pipeline rather than a CRM field.

A standout is the Historical Headcount API, which lets you track company growth over time — useful for investment, market research, and hiring-signal enrichment. Data ships in JSON, JSONL, CSV, or Parquet with Elasticsearch-style search. The main gotcha is the pricing curve: the Starter tier is essentially a paid trial with a steep jump to higher tiers, and it returns no verified emails, so you'll pair it with a contact enrichment layer for outreach.

Key Features

  • 850M+ employee records - Deep professional and firmographic coverage
  • Multi-source collection - 15+ public sources, cross-referenced and cleaned
  • Historical Headcount API - Track company growth trends over time
  • Frequent refresh - Data updated multiple times per day
  • Flexible formats - JSON, JSONL, CSV, Parquet with search querying

API from $49/month (Starter) – then a jump to roughly $800 (Pro) and $1,500 (Premium). Bulk datasets start around $1,000. Free trial credits available.

✓ Pros

  • Large, well-structured, AI-ready data
  • Excellent for analytics and ML pipelines
  • Historical headcount and hiring signals
  • Fast, well-documented API

✗ Cons

  • Big pricing gap between Starter and Pro
  • No verified emails included
  • Requires engineering to extract value
  • No UI or workflow automation
#11

FullContact

Identity Resolution & Profile Matching at Scale

FullContact takes a different angle on enrichment: instead of just appending fields, it focuses on identity resolution — stitching fragmented records (emails, phones, social handles, device IDs) into a single unified profile. Its Enrich and Resolve APIs are built for teams that need to recognize the same person across messy, duplicated data.

For marketing and data teams wrestling with identity fragmentation, FullContact is purpose-built infrastructure with a strong privacy-and-permissioning story. The trade-offs: pricing is custom and enterprise-leaning, it's heavier to adopt than a simple email lookup, and the identity-graph focus means it's a complement to — not a replacement for — a straightforward contact enrichment source.

Key Features

  • Identity resolution - Unify fragmented records into one profile
  • Enrich & Resolve APIs - Append attributes and match identities
  • Multi-signal matching - Email, phone, social, and device IDs
  • Privacy-first posture - Permission-based data practices
  • Scales to large datasets - Built for enterprise volume

Custom pricing – quote-based, enterprise-leaning. Best for teams solving identity fragmentation rather than simple field append.

✓ Pros

  • Strong identity-resolution capability
  • Great for deduplication and matching
  • Privacy-and-permissioning focus
  • Scales to large datasets

✗ Cons

  • Custom pricing — no public tiers
  • Heavier to adopt than a simple lookup
  • Complements, doesn't replace, contact data
  • Enterprise-leaning fit
#12

Datagma

Lightweight, Affordable Contact Enrichment API

Datagma is a budget-friendly enrichment API that turns a name, email, or LinkedIn URL into a fuller contact and company record. It's a popular pick for indie developers and small teams who want a simple endpoint for appending emails, phones, and firmographics without committing to an enterprise platform.

The draw is accessibility: there's a free tier and paid plans start low (around $39/month), with straightforward credit-based usage. The trade-offs match the price point — coverage and match rate trail the larger providers, documentation is leaner, and it's best as a low-cost enrichment layer rather than the backbone of a high-volume operation.

Key Features

  • Multi-input enrichment - Name, email, or LinkedIn URL to a full record
  • Contact + company append - Emails, phones, and firmographics
  • Simple REST API - Quick to wire into a workflow
  • Low entry price - Accessible for indie and small teams
  • Free tier - Light credits to test

Free tier available – paid plans from around $39/month, credit-based. Higher tiers add volume and features.

✓ Pros

  • Very affordable, with a free tier
  • Flexible inputs (name, email, LinkedIn)
  • Simple, fast to integrate
  • Good for low-volume enrichment

✗ Cons

  • Coverage and match rate trail leaders
  • Leaner documentation
  • Not built for high-volume backbone use

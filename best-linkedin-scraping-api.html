10 Best LinkedIn Scraping APIs in 2026

We tested every LinkedIn scraping API on the market — measuring accuracy, account-ban risk, rate limits, response time, and real per-lookup cost. Here's the honest ranking, with our top pick at the top.

Most LinkedIn scraping APIs sound the same on the homepage — "real-time data," "millions of profiles," "99% accuracy." Once you actually plug them in, the differences are brutal: emails are stale, profiles fail silently, rate limits crater your workflows, or worse — the API uses your own LinkedIn account in the background and gets it permanently banned.

A good LinkedIn scraping API should give you four things at once: verified data (real emails, current job titles, accurate company info), zero account risk (it never touches your personal LinkedIn login), predictable pricing (no surprise overage bills), and real integrations (works with n8n, Make, Zapier, your CRM, your scripts). Most APIs nail one or two of those. Very few nail all four.

We tested 18 LinkedIn scraping APIs over 4 months on real B2B prospecting workloads — profile lookups, company employee extraction, email enrichment, post reaction scraping. We compared accuracy on the same 500-record dataset, measured response time under load, and tracked which providers triggered LinkedIn account warnings vs. operated safely on their own infrastructure. The 10 APIs below survived the cut.

Proxycurl

Developer-Friendly LinkedIn API with Cached Profile Data

Proxycurl is one of the best-known LinkedIn data APIs among developers, offering clean documentation, predictable pricing, and a simple credit-based system. Like LinkFinder, it doesn't use your personal LinkedIn account — instead, it pulls from a cached and re-crawled dataset of public LinkedIn profiles.

The platform exposes endpoints for person lookup, company lookup, employee search, and email finding. It's a popular pick for solo developers and small SaaS teams building data products. The trade-off vs. real-time APIs is data freshness: Proxycurl's profile data can be days or weeks old, which matters if you're qualifying leads on current job title or recent company changes.

Key Features

  • Person and company endpoints — Profile lookup, employee search, contact info enrichment
  • Cached dataset — Pulls from pre-crawled LinkedIn data, no live scraping
  • Credit-based pricing — Pay only for successful lookups, with transparent per-call costs
  • Clean developer documentation — One of the best-documented LinkedIn APIs in the market
  • Free credits to test — Generous free tier for evaluation
  • No personal account required — Zero risk to your LinkedIn login

Starting at $49/month — credit-based pricing with per-lookup costs ranging from $0.01 to $0.05 depending on endpoint. Pay-as-you-go available for higher volumes.

✓ Pros

  • Clean developer experience and documentation
  • Predictable credit-based pricing
  • Strong reputation in the developer community

#4

Apify LinkedIn Actors

Marketplace of Community-Built LinkedIn Scrapers — High Account Risk

Apify isn't a single LinkedIn scraping API — it's a marketplace of community-built "Actors" that scrape LinkedIn in various ways. There are dozens of LinkedIn-related actors covering profile scraping, company employee extraction, post scraping, and Sales Navigator data. Most of them work by automating a real LinkedIn account in the background.

This is where it gets risky: many Apify LinkedIn actors require you to provide your own LinkedIn session cookie. The actor then uses that account to scrape, which means LinkedIn sees automated activity coming from your personal profile. Get rate-limited, get warned, eventually get banned. We've seen multiple customers migrate to LinkFinder after losing LinkedIn accounts to Apify-based workflows.

Key Features

  • Marketplace of LinkedIn actors — Dozens of community-built scrapers
  • Flexible pay-per-use pricing — Pay only for compute and data you consume
  • Integration with Apify platform — Webhooks, schedulers, datasets, proxy management
  • Custom actor development — Build your own scraper if existing ones don't fit
  • Apify Store community — Active developer ecosystem

Pay-per-use pricing — typical workloads range from $30-$300/month depending on actor and volume. Compute units charged separately from data transfer.

✓ Pros

  • Huge variety of LinkedIn-specific actors
  • Flexible pay-per-use pricing model
  • Strong developer ecosystem and tooling
  • Custom actor development possible
  • Good integration capabilities (webhooks, schedulers)

✗ Cons

  • High LinkedIn account ban risk — most actors use your personal cookie
  • Quality varies wildly between community actors
  • Many actors break when LinkedIn updates its UI
  • Compute pricing model is hard to predict
  • No SLA on data quality or accuracy
#5

PhantomBuster

Popular No-Code LinkedIn Automation — Uses Your Personal LinkedIn Account

PhantomBuster is one of the most widely used LinkedIn automation tools, especially among growth marketers and sales ops teams. It offers pre-built "Phantoms" that scrape LinkedIn profiles, send connection requests, extract Sales Navigator data, and automate engagement. The UI is friendly and the documentation is solid.

The critical caveat is the same as Apify: PhantomBuster works by automating your real LinkedIn account via a session cookie. Run it cautiously and within sensible daily limits, and most users are fine. Push it too hard or run multiple Phantoms in parallel, and LinkedIn flags or restricts your account. For sensitive workflows — especially anything involving your main outreach LinkedIn — this risk is meaningful.

Key Features

  • 50+ LinkedIn Phantoms — Profile scraping, Sales Navigator export, auto-connect, message automation
  • No-code workflow builder — Chain Phantoms together with Flows
  • Cloud-based execution — No need to keep your machine on
  • CRM integrations — Connects to HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive
  • Strong documentation — Good tutorials and use-case guides

Starting at $69/month — includes 20 hours of execution time. Higher tiers up to $439/month for heavier usage and more concurrent Phantoms.

✓ Pros

  • Friendly no-code interface for non-developers
  • Wide selection of LinkedIn Phantoms covering many use cases
  • Cloud execution, no local setup needed
  • Solid documentation and customer support
  • CRM integrations save manual work

✗ Cons

  • Uses your personal LinkedIn account — real ban risk
  • Strict daily limits to stay safe (slows down workloads)
  • Execution-time pricing penalizes long-running jobs
  • Phantoms break when LinkedIn changes its UI
  • Not suitable for high-volume scraping

Coresignal

Bulk LinkedIn Data for Enterprise Data Science Teams

Coresignal is a B2B data provider that licenses large bulk datasets of LinkedIn-style professional profiles, plus an enrichment API for individual lookups. It's positioned toward data science teams, market intelligence platforms, and enterprises that need millions of records for analysis rather than per-lead prospecting.

Their data covers professional profiles, company firmographics, and job postings, refreshed on a rolling schedule. Because it's bulk-data rather than live scraping, there's no LinkedIn account risk at all. The trade-off is that pricing is enterprise-tier (custom quotes, typically $1,000+/month minimums) and the API isn't designed for the small-scale, per-lead lookups that most B2B teams need.

Key Features

  • Rolling data refresh — Records re-crawled on a schedule
  • Zero LinkedIn account risk — No live scraping, no proxies, no accounts involved
  • Enterprise compliance — Designed for data licensing and analytics use cases

Custom enterprise pricing — typically starting around $1,000/month for bulk dataset access. Enrichment API priced separately.

Lix

LinkedIn Scraping via Browser Extension + API

Lix is a hybrid LinkedIn scraping tool that combines a Chrome extension with an API. The extension lets you scrape profiles and search results while browsing LinkedIn manually, while the API offers programmatic export of profiles, Sales Navigator searches, and company employee lists.

Because the extension piece relies on the user actively logged into LinkedIn, there's a medium account-ban risk depending on how aggressively it's used. The API side of Lix uses its own infrastructure for some endpoints, but heavier Sales Navigator extractions still rely on logged-in sessions. For light, manual prospecting it's reasonable; for high-volume automated workflows, the risk profile is closer to PhantomBuster.

Key Features

  • Chrome extension + API — Manual scraping plus programmatic access
  • Sales Navigator export — Extract searches from Sales Navigator to CSV/API
  • Per-record credit pricing — Simple credit-based consumption
  • Email finder add-on — Verified business email enrichment
  • Free trial credits — Test before subscribing

Starting at $49/month — credit-based pricing. Email finder typically priced as an add-on per credit.

✓ Pros

  • Useful hybrid of extension + API for flexible workflows
  • Sales Navigator export is well-supported
  • Reasonable pricing for small-team usage
  • Simple credit model

✗ Cons

  • Extension usage exposes your LinkedIn account to risk
  • Sales Navigator extraction relies on logged-in sessions
  • Email accuracy below top-tier providers
  • Smaller team, less mature API surface
#8

ScrapingBee

General-Purpose Scraping API (Including LinkedIn) — Requires Your Own Setup

ScrapingBee is a general-purpose web scraping API that handles proxies, headless browsers, and JavaScript rendering. It can be used to scrape LinkedIn, but unlike dedicated LinkedIn APIs, you provide the URLs and parsing logic yourself — ScrapingBee just handles the request layer.

For LinkedIn specifically, this means you're responsible for figuring out which URLs to hit, parsing the HTML response, and handling LinkedIn's anti-bot defenses (which are aggressive). It's a flexible tool for developers who want maximum control, but it's not a turn-key LinkedIn API. Most users find that dedicated LinkedIn APIs deliver cleaner results for less engineering work.

Key Features

  • General-purpose scraping API — Works for LinkedIn and any other site
  • Headless browser rendering — Handles JavaScript-heavy pages
  • Premium proxy network — Residential and datacenter options
  • Country-level geo-targeting — Useful for region-specific LinkedIn data
  • Generous free tier — 1,000 free API credits to start

Starting at $49/month — 150,000 API credits, with LinkedIn requests typically consuming 25 credits each due to premium proxy + JS rendering.

✓ Pros

  • Flexible — works for any site, not just LinkedIn
  • Strong proxy and rendering infrastructure
  • Good for technical teams who want full control
  • Generous free tier for evaluation
  • Doesn't require your personal LinkedIn account

✗ Cons

  • You handle all parsing logic yourself
  • LinkedIn anti-bot defenses still cause failures
  • Credit cost per LinkedIn page is high
  • No verified email enrichment included
  • Significant engineering work to build a real prospecting workflow

RapidAPI LinkedIn Scrapers

Marketplace of Third-Party LinkedIn APIs — Quality Varies Wildly

RapidAPI hosts dozens of third-party LinkedIn scraping APIs from independent developers. You'll find profile lookup APIs, email finders, company scrapers, and post extractors, with prices ranging from $10 to $200/month depending on the provider.

The advantage is variety and low entry pricing. The downside is that quality varies enormously. Many of these APIs are maintained by solo developers, with no SLA, no support response time, and frequent breakage when LinkedIn updates its UI. Some are reliable; many are not. Use only after thorough testing on your real data, and never as the sole backbone of a production workflow.

Key Features

  • Marketplace of dozens of LinkedIn APIs — Wide variety of providers and endpoints
  • Low entry pricing — Some plans as cheap as $10/month
  • Unified billing through RapidAPI — Manage multiple APIs in one place
  • Free tiers commonly available — Test before subscribing
  • API discovery — Browse and compare alternatives easily

Pricing varies by provider — typically $10-$200/month depending on the API and request volume.

✓ Pros

  • Very low entry pricing for testing
  • Wide variety of LinkedIn endpoints available
  • Unified billing across multiple providers
  • Easy to compare alternatives

✗ Cons

  • Quality and accuracy vary wildly between providers
  • Many APIs are maintained by solo devs with no SLA
  • Frequent breakage when LinkedIn updates its UI
  • Limited customer support
  • Risk of API disappearing without notice

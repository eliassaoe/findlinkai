12 Best Email Finder APIs in 2026

Looking for the best email finder API? We compared the top tools for turning a name, domain, or LinkedIn URL into a verified work email — ranked on match rate, verification, deliverability, and real starting price, so you can enrich and reach contacts without bouncing.

Every email finder API promises the same thing — give it a name and a company and get back a valid work email. The differences that actually matter are buried one layer down: match rate (how often it returns an email at all), accuracy (whether that email is real and current), and verification (whether it tells you the address is deliverable before you send to it). A finder with a 90% match rate is useless if a third of those addresses bounce and torch your sender reputation.

Pricing models vary just as much. Some tools charge per search whether or not they find anything; the better ones only bill on a verified hit. Some bundle verification for free; others charge separately. And "credits" rarely map 1:1 to results — a single enriched contact can quietly cost two or three credits once you add verification and extra fields.

We compared the email finder APIs that actually deliver in 2026 across what matters most: match rate, verification quality, deliverability, developer experience, and real starting price. The 12 below are the ones worth your time. The short version: if you want verified work emails with flat, predictable pricing and an API on every plan, you can skip the credit math entirely.

The Best-Known Email Finder & Verifier

Hunter is the category's reference point for one specific job: turning a name and domain into a verified work email. Its Email Finder, Domain Search, and Email Verifier endpoints are clean, fast, and well documented, making it a default choice for developers who want reliable email finding without buying a whole sales platform.

The appeal is focus and price: there's a free tier for light use, and paid plans start around $34/month with predictable monthly credits. The trade-off is scope — Hunter is excellent at emails and verification but isn't a deep firmographic or phone-number source, and match rates on smaller or non-Western domains can dip, so larger operations sometimes pair it with a second finder.

Profile Enrichment That Includes Emails at Scale

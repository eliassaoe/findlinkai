First, the honest part: these aren't the same tool

It's worth being upfront, because most "alternative" pages aren't. NinjaPear is a B2B company intelligence API built by Nubela — the same team behind Proxycurl. Its flagship is the Customer Listing endpoint, which tells you which companies are customers of a given business. That's competitive and partnership intelligence at the company level, and it's a genuinely useful, fairly unique capability.

LinkFinder AI is a people-and-contact platform: look up LinkedIn profiles, enrich contacts, find work emails, and discover leads by describing them in plain English. There's overlap on company data, but the core jobs are different.

So who's actually on this page? Usually one of two people: someone who tried NinjaPear for profile or contact lookup and found it's built for something else — or someone who hit NinjaPear's 12-month commitment and wants a tool they can pay for monthly. If that's you, LinkFinder AI is a clean fit. If you specifically need company-customer mapping, NinjaPear is the specialist and we'll say so.

The pricing reality that sends people looking

NinjaPear's pricing is where most NinjaPear-alternative searches start. Two things tend to surprise people:

1. The 12-month commitment. All NinjaPear plans require a 12-month commitment. Canceling a "monthly" subscription early can trigger fees equal to the remaining months on the contract. The free trial is 3 days with 10 credits — enough for a call or two, not a real evaluation before committing to a year.

2. Credit math that compounds. Plans scale up to around $1,899/month, with ala-carte credits about $0.009 each on the top tier. But several endpoints charge per request plus per result. The flagship Customer Listing endpoint costs 1 credit per request + 2 credits per company returned — so a query that returns 15 companies is 31 credits (~$0.28), and you're charged even when a query comes back empty. Subscription credits also expire each billing cycle.

LinkFinder AI is the opposite shape: 1 credit = 1 request on any endpoint, billed monthly with no lock-in, and 25 free credits to start. No per-result multipliers, no 12-month contract, no remaining-months cancellation fee. See the pricing page for current limits.

Switching is light (for profile & contact use cases)

If your NinjaPear use is profile or contact enrichment, migration is quick — both are REST + JSON. LinkFinder requests go to one endpoint with a type field:

curl -X POST "https://api.linkfinderai.com" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \

NinjaPear alternative FAQ

What is NinjaPear?
A B2B company intelligence API built by Nubela, the same team behind Proxycurl. It launched in 2026 with the Customer Listing endpoint as its flagship — an API that returns which companies are customers of a given business. Its focus is company-level intelligence, not individual profile or email lookup.
Is NinjaPear the same company as Proxycurl?
Yes — both are made by Nubela. After Proxycurl shut down in 2025 following a LinkedIn lawsuit, Nubela launched NinjaPear, and the Customer Listing endpoint that's now NinjaPear's flagship previously lived under Proxycurl. Nubela points former Proxycurl users toward NinjaPear.
Is NinjaPear a good replacement for Proxycurl profile lookups?
Only partly. Most developers used Proxycurl for profile scraping and contact enrichment, which isn't NinjaPear's focus — NinjaPear is company/customer intelligence. For the people-and-contact side, a profile-focused API like LinkFinder AI is a closer match. See our migrate from Proxycurl guide.
How much does NinjaPear cost in 2026?
Credit-based plans scaling up to ~$1,899/month, with ala-carte credits around $0.009 each on the top tier. All plans require a 12-month commitment, and the free trial is 3 days / 10 credits.
Does NinjaPear require a contract?
Yes. Public reviews report all plans require a 12-month commitment, and canceling a "monthly" plan early can incur fees equal to the remaining contract months. Subscription credits expire each cycle; pay-as-you-go credits last about 18 months.
How does NinjaPear's credit pricing work?
Per credit, with some endpoints adding per-result charges. Customer Listing is 1 credit per request + 2 credits per company returned — so 15 companies = 31 credits (~$0.28), and you're charged even on empty results. LinkFinder AI is flat: 1 credit = 1 request, billed monthly.
What's a good NinjaPear alternative?
For profiles, contacts, and lead finding rather than customer-mapping: LinkFinder AI — 1 credit per request, monthly billing, no 12-month lock-in, 25 free credits, and natural-language lead search. For company-customer intelligence specifically, NinjaPear is the specialist.
Can I move from NinjaPear to LinkFinder AI easily?
For profile/contact use cases, yes — both are REST APIs taking JSON. Point requests at https://api.linkfinderai.com, set the type field, pass a Bearer key, and read status/result. Customer Listing has no direct equivalent. See the API documentation.

12 Best LinkedIn APIs in 2026

Looking for the best LinkedIn API? We compared the top profile, company, and contact-data APIs to help you pull clean LinkedIn data for lookup, enrichment, and automation — without getting your account banned.

LinkedIn doesn't make this easy. The official API is gated behind a partner program that takes months to get into and rejects most applicants — and even when you're approved, it barely exposes the profile and company data most teams actually want. So almost everyone ends up reaching for a third-party LinkedIn API instead.

We compared the providers that actually work in 2026 across what matters most: data coverage, freshness, account-ban risk, ease of integration, and real starting price. The 12 below are the ones worth your time. #2

LinkedIn Official API

The Official Route — Compliant, Free, and Heavily Restricted

LinkedIn's official developer platform is the only fully sanctioned way to access LinkedIn data, and it's free for approved partners. The catch is approval: most data-access products live behind the Marketing Developer Platform or partner programs, where applications can take three to six months and rejections are common — especially for anything that resembles bulk data collection.

Where it shines is the things LinkedIn officially blesses: posting and scheduling content, "Sign in with LinkedIn," and approved marketing integrations. For deep profile reads, employee lists, or search at scale, the official API simply isn't built to deliver — and premium products like Sales Navigator and Recruiter APIs carry their own licensing fees on top.

Key Features

  • Fully compliant - The only first-party, fully sanctioned access path
  • Sign in with LinkedIn - OAuth identity and basic profile fields
  • Content & marketing APIs - Post, schedule, and manage ads for approved partners
  • Zero account risk - Official tokens, no scraping, no cookies
  • Sales Navigator / Recruiter APIs - Available to qualifying partners with licensing

Free for approved partners – there's no public price list. Real costs come from the approval effort, plus separate licensing for Sales Navigator or Recruiter products.

✓ Pros

  • 100% compliant and first-party
  • No ban risk whatsoever
  • Best choice for posting and ads automation
  • Free for approved use cases

✗ Cons

  • Long, opaque approval process with frequent rejections
  • Very limited profile and company data access
  • No bulk profile reads or search
  • Premium APIs require partner contracts and fees

Bright Data

Enterprise-Grade, Compliant LinkedIn Datasets at Scale

Bright Data is one of the biggest names in web data, and its LinkedIn offering is built for scale. Rather than running on your account, it delivers structured datasets and scraper APIs for profiles, companies, jobs, and posts — either as prebuilt datasets or custom pulls tailored to your spec.

It's a popular choice for teams that need large volumes of accurate, current data with a compliance story attached. Delivery is flexible — you can pipe data into Amazon S3, Snowflake, or Google Cloud — and pricing works out to roughly five cents per profile at volume. The trade-off is that it's an enterprise platform: powerful, but heavier and pricier than a simple REST endpoint, and occasional downtime gets reported during large runs.

Key Features

  • Profiles, companies, jobs & posts - Full LinkedIn coverage as datasets or API
  • Prebuilt or custom datasets - Order off-the-shelf or spec your own
  • Flexible delivery - S3, Snowflake, Google Cloud, and more
  • Compliance focus - Built around public data with documented practices
  • No account risk - Managed collection, not your cookies
  • Scales to millions of records - Built for enterprise volume

Around $0.05 per profile – usage-based at scale, with custom enterprise agreements. Expect higher effective costs on small volumes and add-ons.

✓ Pros

  • Massive, accurate LinkedIn coverage
  • No account-ban exposure
  • Strong compliance posture
  • Flexible cloud delivery options
  • Battle-tested at enterprise scale

✗ Cons

  • Expensive and complex for small teams

  • Some downtime reported on large jobs
  • Overkill if you just need a few thousand records

Coresignal

AI-Ready Employee, Company & Job Datasets via API

Coresignal is an API-first data platform aimed at engineers, analysts, and AI teams. It collects from 15+ public sources and delivers structured, deduplicated records — hundreds of millions of employee profiles plus tens of millions of company records, with data refreshed multiple times a day.

A standout is the Historical Headcount API, which lets you track company growth over time — useful for investment, market research, and hiring-signal use cases. Data ships in JSON, JSONL, CSV, or Parquet with Elasticsearch-style search. The main gotcha is the pricing curve: the Starter tier is essentially a paid trial, and there's a steep jump to the higher tiers, with no mid-range option in between. It also returns no verified emails, so you'll pair it with an enrichment layer for outreach.

Key Features

  • 850M+ employee records - Deep professional and firmographic coverage
  • Multi-source collection - 15+ public sources, cross-referenced and cleaned
  • Historical Headcount API - Track company growth trends over time
  • Frequent refresh - Data updated multiple times per day
  • Flexible formats - JSON, JSONL, CSV, Parquet with search querying
  • No account risk - Dataset and API delivery, not your login

API from $49/month (Starter) – then a jump to roughly $800 (Pro) and $1,500 (Premium). Bulk datasets start around $1,000. A free tier with trial credits is available.

✓ Pros

  • Large, well-structured, AI-ready data
  • Excellent for analytics and ML pipelines
  • Historical headcount and hiring signals
  • Fast, well-documented API

  • Big pricing gap between Starter and Pro tiers
  • No verified emails included

#5

People Data Labs

Massive Person & Company Enrichment API

People Data Labs (PDL) is a B2B data provider with one of the largest person and company datasets around, delivered through clean enrichment and search APIs. Give it an identifier and it returns a structured profile; run a search and it returns matching records — straightforward to integrate into apps and CRMs.

Pricing is credit-based and transparent: person enrichment lands around $0.28 per record and drops toward $0.20 at scale, with company enrichment cheaper. A Pro plan starts near $98/month and there's a free tier with 100 credits to test. The trade-off versus live scrapers is freshness — because PDL serves a compiled dataset, some records can lag behind a person's current role.

Key Features

  • Huge person & company dataset - Among the largest available
  • Enrichment & search APIs - Person and company endpoints
  • Transparent per-record pricing - Roughly $0.28/record, lower at scale
  • Free tier - 100 credits and a free company dataset to evaluate
  • Developer-friendly - Clean schema, easy to integrate
  • No account risk - Compiled dataset, not your login

Pro plan from $98/month (~350 enrichment credits) – or pay-as-you-go at ~$0.28 per person record, ~$0.10 per company record, dropping at volume. Free tier with 100 credits.

✓ Pros

  • Enormous dataset coverage

  • Generous free tier for testing
  • Great for high-volume enrichment

Oxylabs

Enterprise Scraper API That Handles the Hard Targets

Oxylabs is an enterprise scraping infrastructure provider whose Web Scraper API can be pointed at LinkedIn's public pages. It's built to get through aggressive bot detection, with JavaScript rendering, automatic retries, and a large proxy backbone behind it.

That makes it a strong fit for teams that need high success rates on difficult, protected targets and have the engineering chops to wire it up. The flip side is enterprise positioning: pricing is custom and on the premium end, and you'll get the most out of it with technical resources on hand. It's a serious tool for serious volume, not a quick weekend integration.

Key Features

  • High success on protected pages - Advanced anti-bot handling
  • JavaScript rendering - Captures dynamically loaded content
  • Automatic retries - Built-in reliability for large jobs
  • Large proxy backbone - Massive IP infrastructure included
  • Enterprise SLAs - Dedicated support for big customers

Custom enterprise pricing – premium tier, typically quote-based. Best value at higher volumes with technical resources to integrate.

✓ Pros

  • Excellent success rates on tough targets
  • JS rendering and retries built in
  • Enterprise-grade reliability and support
  • No reliance on your LinkedIn account

✗ Cons

  • Expensive and quote-based
  • Requires technical expertise
  • Overkill for small projects
  • You still own parsing and structuring the output
#7

Unipile

Unified Messaging & LinkedIn API for SaaS Builders

Unipile is a unified API layer for messaging — LinkedIn, email, WhatsApp, and more behind a single integration. The sweet spot is building a product that needs to send and receive LinkedIn messages, manage connections, and sync inboxes, all embedded inside your own ATS, CRM, or outreach app.

Pricing is refreshingly simple: it's based on the number of connected accounts (starting around $55/month for up to 10), not per API call or data volume, and there's a 7-day free trial. The important caveat is how it connects: for LinkedIn it operates through reverse engineering on top of a connected account, so it carries genuine account-ban risk — and it's built for messaging and actions, not bulk profile extraction.

Key Features

  • One API, many channels - LinkedIn, email, WhatsApp, and more
  • Send InMails & invitations - Full messaging and connection actions
  • Inbox sync & webhooks - Real-time notifications across channels
  • Account-based pricing - Pay per connected account, not per call
  • Maintained for you - They absorb auth and API changes
  • 7-day free trial - All features, no card required

From ~$55/month (€49) for up to 10 connected accounts, then about $5 per additional account. No per-request charges.

✓ Pros

  • Ideal for embedding messaging into a SaaS
  • Simple, predictable account-based pricing
  • Multi-channel in one integration
  • Handles auth changes for you
  • Webhook support for real-time events

✗ Cons

  • Uses your connected account → real ban risk
  • Built for messaging, not bulk data extraction
  • Not a fit for large-scale profile pulls
  • Costs scale with number of accounts
#8

Apify

Pay-Per-Result Actor Marketplace for LinkedIn Scraping

Apify is a marketplace of cloud scrapers ("Actors") you call via API, and it became a go-to migration path after Proxycurl shut down. Several maintained Actors cover the LinkedIn basics — profile scrapers, company scrapers, and job scrapers — and you pay per result rather than a flat monthly data fee.

That pay-per-event model is its biggest strength: roughly $3 to $10 per 1,000 records on the popular Actors, no long-term commitment, and a free tier to start. Just know the trade-off baked into LinkedIn scraping here — cookieless Actors are safer but shallower, while the deeper ones rely on session cookies and carry account-ban risk. Runs are async, so expect a little latency and a short learning curve on the platform.

Key Features

  • Pay-per-result pricing - ~$3–$10 per 1,000 records, no lock-in
  • Multiple LinkedIn Actors - Profiles, companies, and jobs
  • Cookieless options - Lower-risk Actors that skip session login
  • Free tier - Test before you scale
  • JSON/CSV export - Handles pagination and field mapping
  • Scales linearly - From one profile to a million

Pay-as-you-go – from roughly $3–$10 per 1,000 records depending on the Actor, plus a platform plan from about $25/month. Free tier available.

✓ Pros

  • No monthly commitment on pay-per-event Actors
  • Flexible, scales with usage
  • Cookieless Actors reduce ban risk
  • Big library of maintained scrapers
  • Good free tier for testing

✗ Cons

  • Deeper Actors use cookies → ban risk
  • Async runs add some latency
  • Platform learning curve
  • Data depth varies by Actor

Scrapingdog

Affordable General Scraper API With a LinkedIn Endpoint

Scrapingdog is a general-purpose scraping API that includes a dedicated LinkedIn endpoint for individual profiles and company pages. You can filter by attributes like industry, location, or job title, and results come back as clean JSON ready to drop into your stack.

It's one of the more budget-friendly options, with plans starting around $40/month and a 30-day trial that includes 1,000 free credits. Because LinkedIn is just one of 20+ sites Scrapingdog supports, its LinkedIn data can be a bit inconsistent — education history and skills sections sometimes come back incomplete — and credits expire monthly. For straightforward profile and company reads at a low price, though, it's solid and reliable.

Key Features

  • Dedicated LinkedIn endpoint - Profiles and company pages
  • Attribute filtering - Industry, location, job title
  • Clean JSON output - Easy to integrate
  • Good uptime - Established, reliable infrastructure
  • Multi-site coverage - 20+ sites beyond LinkedIn
  • 1,000 free trial credits - 30-day, no commitment

From $40/month – credit-based, with a 30-day free trial including 1,000 credits. Higher tiers for larger volumes.

✓ Pros

  • Very affordable entry point
  • Reliable uptime
  • Simple JSON responses
  • Handy multi-site coverage
  • Generous trial credits

✗ Cons

  • LinkedIn data can have gaps (education, skills)
  • Credits expire monthly
  • Monthly commitment required
  • LinkedIn isn't the core focus

Xverum

Fresh People, Company & Job Datasets With API Refresh

Xverum specializes in bulk LinkedIn-style datasets — people, companies, and job postings — delivered as raw data with an API refresh service to keep it current. Teams that buy from Xverum tend to praise the accuracy of the raw data and the refresh cadence relative to other dataset vendors.

It's best suited to analytics, lead databases, and AI training pipelines where you want large volumes of structured records rather than one-off live lookups. Like other dataset providers, it sidesteps account-ban risk entirely. The trade-offs are that pricing is custom and dataset-oriented, and it's less of a plug-and-play REST API than a data-feed relationship.

Key Features

  • API refresh service - Keeps purchased data current
  • High raw-data accuracy - Praised refresh rate and quality
  • Bulk delivery - Built for analytics and AI workflows
  • No account risk - Dataset-based, not your login

Custom dataset pricing – scoped to volume, coverage, and refresh frequency. Best for teams buying data at scale rather than ad-hoc lookups.

✓ Pros

#11

PhantomBuster

Cookie-Based Automation for Data Plus Actions

PhantomBuster is an automation platform that runs "Phantoms" against LinkedIn and 20+ other sites. Beyond pulling profile data, it can perform actions — auto-connecting, messaging, and following — which makes it popular with sales teams running light outbound.

The important thing to understand is that it works through your own LinkedIn session cookies, so it carries a real account-ban risk, and LinkedIn limits how much one account can do per day. Pricing starts at $69/month, billed across three separate quotas — execution hours, Phantom slots, and email credits — that all expire monthly, so real-world cost can climb faster than the sticker price. Great for hands-on outreach; not the tool for clean, large-scale data extraction.

Key Features

  • Data + actions - Scrape, connect, message, and follow
  • 20+ integrations - LinkedIn, X, Sheets, CRMs, and more
  • Workspace pricing - Multiple LinkedIn accounts per workspace
  • Prebuilt Phantoms - Ready-made automation recipes
  • Sequencing - Chain actions into outreach flows

From $69/month (Starter) – with Pro and Team tiers above. Watch the three separate quotas (execution hours, slots, email credits) that expire each cycle.

✓ Pros

  • Performs actions, not just data pulls
  • Big library of prebuilt automations
  • Per-workspace pricing for small teams
  • Good for light, hands-on outbound

✗ Cons

  • Runs on your cookies → high ban risk
  • Daily limits constrain volume
  • Three expiring quotas complicate budgeting
  • Real cost often exceeds the headline price
#12

RapidAPI Marketplace Scrapers

Cheap, Quick LinkedIn Endpoints for Prototyping

The RapidAPI marketplace hosts dozens of independent LinkedIn scraper APIs from different developers, usually with a free or low-cost tier. For quick prototyping or a one-off script, you can be pulling profile JSON within minutes and pay very little.

The catch is consistency. Because these are third-party listings rather than a single maintained service, reliability, data quality, and uptime vary widely from one API to the next, and there's typically no real SLA. When LinkedIn changes something, an individual listing can break or vanish overnight. They're useful for testing an idea — but for production, you'll usually graduate to one of the dedicated providers above.

Key Features

  • Many providers in one place - Dozens of LinkedIn endpoints
  • Low cost to start - Free and cheap tiers common
  • Fast to try - Subscribe and call in minutes
  • JSON responses - Standard marketplace format
  • Variety of use cases - Profiles, companies, search, jobs

Pricing varies – from free tiers up to a few hundred dollars a month depending on the listing. No unified pricing or SLA across providers.

✓ Pros

  • Cheapest way to start
  • Fast for prototyping and testing
  • Lots of options to compare
  • Standard JSON output

✗ Cons

  • Inconsistent quality and uptime
  • No real SLA or support
  • Listings can break or disappear
  • Not reliable for production
